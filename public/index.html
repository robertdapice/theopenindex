TheOpenIndex.org

Australia's leaderboard of government transparency, revealing which government departments are the most (and least) transparent.

Each department's Open Index rates their commitment to transparency and open data.

TheOpenIndex is built from 5 components.

  • Open Data scores departments on the quantity and quality of data they release onto data.gov.au.
  • Financial Reporting scores departments on the level of financial detail provided in their Portfolio Budget Statements.
  • FOI Compliance scores departments on the quantity of FOI requests that are resolved within statutory guidelines.
  • Online Access scores departments on the performance and accessibility of their website, and their responsiveness to Twitter mentions.
  • Overdue Questions-on-Notice scores departments on how many questions from senate estimate hearings are not overdue.

Click on any category to explore further.

Open Data

Departments should release data by third parties to enable novel services and insights.

TheOpenIndex rates government departments on their contribution to the data.gov.au, and the new data.gov.au beta.

Departments are scored on the number of their datasets available and the average hits per day, rating and votes for their datasets.

Source: data.gov.au

Financial Reporting

Departments should report in detail where and how they spend their money.

TheOpenIndex uses data from TheOpenBudget to analyse the level of detail each Portfolio provides in their Portfolio Budget Statements.

Portfolios are scored based on how much of their total expenditure is reported in line items that are smaller than 15% of their total expenditure.

Source: TheOpenBudget.org

FOI Compliance

Departments should respond to FOI requests within 30 days, as per their statutory obligations.

TheOpenIndex rates each department on the proportion of FOI requests that are not fulfilled within statutory timelines.

Departments receive poorer scores based on both the percentage of FOI requests that are late, and the extent to which they are late. Personal FOI requests are excluded from the analysis.

Source: data.gov.au

Online Access

Departments should provide quality online resources.

TheOpenIndex assesses each Department's web presence using three different factors: performance, accessibility, and twitter responsiveness.

  • Website Performance is scored using the open source tool ySlow.
  • Accessibility is scored using the open source tool WAVE.
  • Twitter Responsiveness is scored using the Twitter API to determine the number of hours it took for the department to respond to @mentions.

Source: TheOpenIndex Calculations

Overdue Questions-on-Notice

Departments should respond to questions from the Senate.

TheOpenIndex assesses the number of questions from the Senate to each department that is overdue, ie have not been answered within 30 days.

Departments with a larger number of overdue questions receive a lower score.

Source: Parliament of Australia website
